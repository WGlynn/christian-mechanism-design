# CMD Orientation: Throne, not Babel

The single rule that keeps Christian Mechanism Design from becoming the thing it
opposes. Read this before any mechanism.

## The error to avoid

CMD does not build mechanisms that replace the sacraments. A mechanism that
tried to be confession, or to stand in for grace, or to mediate between a person
and God, would be a false idol. It would bastardize the faith by putting code
where the holy act belongs. That is the Tower of Babel: man building a structure
to reach the throne and take the seat.

We are not doing that.

## The orientation

CMD codes the morals of Jesus' sermons into the intent behind a mechanism. The
teaching shapes the design. The design serves people in the secular work of
coordination: markets, governance, communities, shared resources. The mechanism
never claims the sacred function, and never asks to be trusted the way the
sacrament is trusted.

The order runs one direction only:

    sermon  ->  moral principle  ->  design intent  ->  mechanism

Never the reverse. The mechanism is downstream and subordinate. It points back
toward the throne. It does not sit on it.

This is the same discipline as augmenting an invariant instead of replacing it.
The sacrament is the invariant. We build around it, informed by it, never over
it.

## Worked correction

Take confession. The wrong move is "confession as a commit-reveal scheme," a
digital sacrament. That is Babel.

The right move is to ask what confession teaches: honest self-accounting,
repentance, and reconciliation before you bring your gift to the altar (Matthew
5:23-24). Then carry that moral as the intent into an ordinary mechanism. For
example, a dispute process for a shared treasury that requires the parties to
reconcile, in good faith and on the record, before a contested proposal can be
funded. No one confesses to the code. No grace is claimed. The mechanism is just
a secular tool whose shape was chosen because the moral is true.

## Two things that make this strong

1. You do not have to be a Christian to want Christians, or anyone, to act
   better. The mechanisms are useful to a designer of any faith or none. Their
   value does not depend on the designer's belief, only on whether the moral
   they encode is true.

2. There can be a mechanism-design family for any moral tradition. Christian
   Mechanism Design is one instance. Christianity is the tradition the author
   puts his faith in, so it is where the work starts and where the bet is
   placed. The method generalizes. The starting point is a confession of faith,
   not a claim of exclusivity.
