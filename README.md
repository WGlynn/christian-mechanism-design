# Christian Mechanism Design (CMD)

A variant of Augmented Mechanism Design. CMD is the claim that Christian moral
commitment and mechanism design improve each other, because each closes a
structural gap the other cannot reach alone.

It is not a religious argument. It is the logical conclusion of taking both
seriously at the same time.

## The two gaps

**Mechanism design cannot reach intent.**
Economic and on-chain mechanisms enforce only what is observable and
contractible. They cannot see the will, the private utility function, or the
off-chain act. This is the airgap: the chain is orthogonal to reality, so
incentive enforcement has a ceiling no clever design fully removes. The agent's
true preferences stay private.

**Christianity cannot, by exhortation alone, close the gap between word and
deed.**
The oldest critique of the faith comes from inside it. "This people honors me
with their lips, but their heart is far from me" (Matthew 15:8). The believer
professes one utility function, honesty and love and sacrifice, and reveals
another under pressure. Romans 7: "the good that I would I do not." Exhortation
has a ceiling too.

## Why they fit

The two ceilings are the same shape, pointed in opposite directions.

**Christianity supplies what mechanism design lacks: an internalized enforcer.**
Taking up one's cross (Matthew 16:24) is a voluntary, costly, internalized
pre-commitment. A believer who has genuinely bound their utility function to
conscience before God carries the enforcement the chain can never install. That
closes the airgap from the human side. In Augmented Mechanism Design's
accountability stack, Physics over Constitution over Government, CMD adds the
layer above physics: conscience and final accountability reach exactly where
physics cannot, into intent and the secret act.

**Mechanism design supplies what Christianity lacks: structure that makes
hypocrisy unprofitable.**
A good mechanism does by design what exhortation cannot do by appeal.

- Commit-reveal makes you stake your word before the outcome is known, so
  profession precedes result.
- Costly signaling separates sincere from performative faith. The cost that
  filters for sincerity is the same cost that signals it. The filter and the
  signal coincide.
- Shapley-fair attribution rewards actual contribution over professed status.
  This is structurally anti-Pharisee.
- "By their fruits you shall know them" (Matthew 7:16) is revealed-preference
  verification, which is a mechanism, stated two thousand years early.

The believer gets external structure that pulls revealed behavior toward
professed values. Hypocrisy is overcome by design, not only by willpower.

## The loop

- Christianity to mechanism design: provides the off-chain enforcer that
  dissolves the airgap.
- Mechanism design to Christianity: provides the structure that dissolves
  hypocrisy.

Each makes the other reach further. That is the bidirectional improvement.

## Scripture as mechanism primitives

| Passage | Mechanism primitive |
|---|---|
| Matthew 5:37, let your yes be yes | Honesty as a load-bearing structural property |
| Matthew 16:24, take up your cross | Voluntary costly commitment device |
| Matthew 7:16, by their fruits | Revealed-preference verification |
| Matthew 6:1-4, do not let your left hand know | Privacy and commit-reveal of good works, not signaling for status |
| Matthew 25:14-30, the talents | Stewardship and Shapley attribution |
| Matthew 22:21, render unto Caesar | Layered, separated governance |

## Guardrails

CMD augments, it does not coerce. The discipline is inherited from Augmented
Mechanism Design: augment an invariant, never replace freedom or conviction.

- Voluntary only. The cross is taken up, never imposed.
- A mechanism reveals and aligns. It does not compel belief.
- Metaphysics-agnostic at the engineering layer. CMD works wherever the
  commitment is genuinely internalized. It does not require settling theological
  truth to function, which is what keeps it from being either proselytizing or
  cynical.
